Series Notes

Nocturnes

A sequence of oil paintings exploring the liminal space between dusk and full night. Each canvas uses a restricted palette of indigo, charcoal, and muted golds to capture the quiet tension of transitional light. Scale ranges from intimate (18×24 inches) to expansive (48×60 inches), with brushwork that balances gestural energy and meditative stillness.

Urban Fragments

Mixed-media drawings on heavyweight paper combining graphite, ink, and collage elements. This series documents architectural details and overlooked urban textures—fire escapes, weathered signage, industrial grids. Materials include found ephemera and hand-drawn line work, creating layered compositions that oscillate between abstraction and representation.

Threshold Sculptures

Three-dimensional works constructed from wood, metal, and found objects. These sculptures investigate boundaries and passage—physical and metaphorical. Ranging from tabletop scale (12×8×6 inches) to room-sized installations, each piece invites circumnavigation and shifting perspectives. Surfaces are left raw or subtly patinated to honor material history.

Chromatic Studies

A research-driven series of acrylic paintings on panel exploring color interaction and optical vibration. Working within a square format (24×24 inches), each piece isolates two or three hues in precise geometric arrangements. The result is a meditation on perception, afterimage, and the emotional resonance of color relationships.
